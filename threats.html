Threats to Red Pandas

Red pandas are endangered, with fewer than 10,000 individuals left in the wild. Their population has declined by 50% over the last 20 years due to various threats.

Habitat Loss

Deforestation for agriculture, logging, and development has destroyed much of the red panda's natural habitat. Their forest homes are being fragmented, making it harder for populations to survive.

Poaching

Red pandas are hunted for their distinctive pelts, which are used to make hats and clothing. They're also captured for the illegal pet trade, despite being poor candidates as pets.

Predation & Competition

Domestic dogs (from nearby villages) often attack red pandas. They also face competition from livestock for food resources and habitat space.

Climate Change

Changing weather patterns affect bamboo growth cycles. Red pandas are highly specialized to their environment and struggle to adapt to rapid changes.
