About Red Pandas

The Forgotten Panda

Red pandas, also known as lesser pandas or firefoxes, are small arboreal mammals native to the eastern Himalayas and southwestern China. Despite their name, they are not closely related to giant pandas.

These adorable creatures were actually the first animals to be called "pandas" - the giant panda was named later due to similarities in their bamboo-based diets.

Physical Characteristics

Red pandas are about the size of a domestic cat, with a body length of 50-64 cm and a tail adding another 28-59 cm. They have soft, dense fur that's reddish-brown on top with a black belly and legs.

Their long, bushy tails help them maintain balance in trees and also serve as warm wrappings in cold weather. They have a specialized wrist bone that acts like a thumb, helping them grasp bamboo.

Behavior and Diet

Red pandas are primarily crepuscular (active at dawn and dusk) and spend most of their time in trees. They are solitary animals, except during mating season.

While they mainly eat bamboo (about two-thirds of their diet), they are technically omnivores and also consume fruits, acorns, roots, eggs, and small animals.
